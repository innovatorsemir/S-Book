Contraction

Contraction :- is a shortened form of a group of words. Contractions are used in both written and oral communication.

⇒ When a contraction is written in English, the omitted letters are replaced by an apostrophe.

⇒ We will see the Common (Formal) contractions and Informal contractions.



1. Formal Contractions / ስሩዕ ምሕፃረ-ቃላት

⇒ Here are some common contractions and the groups of words that they represent.

Note: One contraction that is not on the above list is “it’s”. It is useful to note that “it’s”, a contraction, is often confused with “its”, a possessive pronoun. Remember,
⇒ it’s → it is
⇒ its → possessive pronoun
⇒ Examples of It’s and Its
E.g:1) It’s nice outside today. = It is nice outside today.
E.g:2) The dog ate its food. (The food belongs to the dog.)



1. Informal Contractions / ዘይስሩዕ ምሕፃረ-ቃላት

⇒ American English Informal contractions are shortened forms of words and phrases in English. They are like slang. Slang is a very informal way of talking.
⇒ This short form is often used in speaking and in some informal writing like texts, emails, and social media..

Examples: Contractions with of words

kinda → kind of sorta → sort of
outta → out of lotta → lot of
cuppa → cup of much of → mucha
