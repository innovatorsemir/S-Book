4. Conditional Tense

⇒ The conditional tenses in English are used:

  • to talk something about to happen or happened based on condition

⇒ There are two conditional tense forms in English:

Conditional Tenses: Examples
A) Present conditional: I would eat
B) Past conditional: I would have eaten

⇒ We use these forms:

  • to talk based on conditions:

E.g: He works at McDonald’s. He has worked there for three months now.
→ He is working at McDonald’s. He has been working there for three months now.
→ London is the capital of Britain.

  • to talk about the future:

E.g: The next train leaves this evening at 1700 hours.
→ I’ll phone you when I get home.
→ He’s meeting Peter in town this afternoon.
→ I’ll come home as soon as I have finished work.
→ You will be tired out after you have been working all night.

  • We can use the present tenses to talk about the past...

⇒ I have just eaten my lunch.


A. Present Conditional Tense / ህልዊ ኲነታዊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:-
S + would/should/could/might + V1 + O

⇒ OR
S + have to + V1 + O

⇒ We use the present tense to talk about:

  • something about to happen based on condition

E.g: You should study hard.
→ He mightn't come in.
→ Would you mind opening the door.

B. Past Conditional Tense / ሕሉፍ ኲነታዊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:-
S + would/should/could/might + have + V3 + O

⇒ OR
S + had to + V1 + O

⇒ We use the past conditional tense to talk about:

  • something about to happened based on condition

E.g: You should have studied hard..
→ I should have had money.
