3. Future Tense

⇒ The future tenses in English are used:

  • to talk about the future

⇒ There are four future tense forms in English:

Future Tenses: Examples
A) Future simple: I will eat
B) Future continuous: I will be eating
C) Future perfect: I will have eaten
D) Future perfect continuous: I will have been eating

⇒ We use these forms:

  • to talk about an action that will happen in future:

E.g: Kibrom will teach us.
→ She will have been working there next day.
→ I will be doing my home work.


A. Future Simple Tense / ቀሊል ህልዊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:- S + will/shall + V1 + O

⇒ The simple future tense is the will + base form of the verb. E.g: I will work in London.

⇒ And the same form with the third person (she/he/it) doesn't add an -s. E.g: She will work in London. (We don't say: She will works in london)

The Usage of Future Simple

⇒ We use the simple future tense to talk about:

  • to talk about an action that will take place in the future:

E.g 1: Zeradawit will be here tomorrow. (Prediction about future)

E.g 2: Tonight's programme will be very interesting. (Prediction about future)
E.g 3: Rain will continue throughout the day. (weather forcoasts)
E.g 4: I expect they will be here soon. (future expectation)
E.g 5: If I get that job, I will go out and celebrate. (condition)
E.g 6: Will you open the door? ( willingness ~request)

  • to express an intention when the decision is made at the time of speaking (suddeen/ moment decision):

E.g: Oh, I've left the door open. I will go and open.
→ I am too tired to go home. I think I'll get a taxi.
→ A: There is not any milk left.
    B: Oh, isn't there? I will get some in the town.

B. Future Continuous Tense / ቀፃሊ ህልዊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:-
S + will/shall + be + Ving + O
⇒ The future continuous tense is formed from the present tense of the verb will be and the present participle (-ing form) of a verb:

The Usage of Future Continuous

⇒ We use the future continuous tense to talk about:

  • I will be doing something = I'll be in the middle of doing something:

E.g 1: The football match begins at 11:30 and ends at 1:30. so during this time, for example at 12:30. I will be watching the match.
E.g 2: Don't phone me between 6 and 7. We will be having lunch.

Note: Compare will be-ing with other continuous form see below examples:

→ At 6 o'clock yesterday, Semir was in his office. (he was working) (past continuous)
→ At 6 o'clock now, Semir is in his office. (he is working) (present continuous)
→ At 6 o'clock tommorrow, Semir will be in his office. (he will be working) (future continuous)

  • Other examples of future continuous tense:

E.g 1: Mengsteab is getting married next month. (future arrangement)

E.g 2: The prime minister is leaving for China next week. (time table)
E.g 3: I am going to visit my home town in my vacation . (future intention)
E.g 4: He hardly studies. He is going to do poor in the exam . (future rediction)

C. Future Perfect Tense / ፍጹም ህልዊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:-
S + will/shall + have + V3 + O

⇒ The future perfect is formed from the present tense of the verb will have and the past participle of a verb:

The Usage of Future Perfect

⇒ We use the future perfect tense:

  • to express something that will be over in the future :

Example:
Aden: What time will you be home?
Elsa: I will have finished here by half past eight, so I should be home about time.

Note: Future perfect is used with future time expressions like:

Examples: Future perfect + by tommorrow, at two morning, by next week, by June, next April, etc
E.g 1: I like looking at the pictures, I'll have had enough by lunch time.
E.g 2: I hope I will have won lots of prizes before I'm twenty.
E.g 3: It's quite a long way, isn't it? We will have walked about five miles by the time we get back, I'd say.
E.g 4: We will have finished this project by the first January.

D. Future Perfect Continuous Tense / ፍጹም ህልዊ ቀፃሊ ግዜ

⇒ Form:-
S + will/shall + have + been + Ving + O

⇒ The future perfect continuous is formed with will/shall + have/has been and the -ing form of the verb:

The Usage of Future Perfect Continuous

⇒ We use the future perfect continuous tense:

  • to describe an activity leading up to a time in the future:

E.g 1: We will have been living here for ten years soon.
E.g 2: They might be tired when you see them because they will have been working hard.
